<p><ul><li>Double Layer</li><li>Pseudocapacitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The supercapacitor market is primarily divided into two types: double layer capacitors and pseudocapacitors. Double layer capacitors store energy through electrostatic charge separation at the interface of an electrode and electrolyte, offering high power density and rapid charging capabilities. In contrast, pseudocapacitors utilize fast surface redox reactions to store energy, providing higher energy density compared to double layer capacitors. Both types play crucial roles in applications requiring quick energy delivery, such as renewable energy systems and electric vehicles.</p></p>
