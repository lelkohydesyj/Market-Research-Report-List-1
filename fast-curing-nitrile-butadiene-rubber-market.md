Cold curing nitrile butadiene rubber is processed at room temperature and often uses accelerators for faster vulcanization, making it suitable for sensitive applications. In contrast, hot curing nitrile butadiene rubber requires elevated temperatures to achieve optimal curing, resulting in enhanced durability and strength. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs, offering specific advantages in terms of processing speed, performance, and application suitability.</p></p>
